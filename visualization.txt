================================================================================
VISUALIZATIONS GUIDE — THE SANDWICH TRAP
================================================================================

HOW TO READ THE VISUALIZATION:
  - Society box (dark): the system — laws, sympathy, media stories
  - Person box (amber): the individual — status, hunger, trust, possesses
  - Orange dashed arrow: what the person TRIED this tick (TryJob/TryAid/TryStreet/GaveUp)
  - Red solid arrows: criminal record — crimes that accumulated from surviving
  - Green arrow: person has the sandwich (goal achieved)
  Use the TIME TAB arrows in Sterling to step tick by tick through the life.

================================================================================
SECTION 1 — THE ROUTES
================================================================================

POOR ROUTES (1–5): All end Homeless, Desperate, no sandwich. Always.
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Route 1 — BORN INTO THE TRAP
  The baseline poor person. No ID, no address, no bank account, no internet.
  All 20 laws active from birth. Decision arrow hits GaveUp almost immediately
  and never moves. Red crime arrows accumulate each tick just from surviving.
  This is the floor. Everything else is a variation on this.

Route 2 — ALMOST MADE IT
  Starts better: precarious housing, a car, fair credit, short resume gap.
  Looks hopeful at birth. Then: car breaks (zero savings to fix it), gap grows
  to AlgorithmFiltered, credit falls, housing lost. Same ending as Route 1.
  The point: a small head start cannot overcome structural barriers.

Route 3 — BORN SICK
  Starts with chronic illness. The insurance paradox fires immediately —
  can't work full-time, so loses employer insurance, so illness worsens,
  so truly can't work. Illness did not cause the trap. The employer-only
  insurance system did. Same ending as Route 1.

Route 4 — ALREADY CHARGED
  Born poor AND already in the legal system (Trespassing on record, Charged).
  Every background check fails at tick 0. Legal defense loop activates:
  no money → no lawyer → assets seized → FailureToProvide charge added.
  Same ending as Route 1, reached faster.

Route 5 — TRIES EVERYTHING (longer trace)
  Same start as Route 1, but Forge finds the most favorable possible trace.
  Even with optimal decisions, every avenue stays closed. Same ending.
  The trap is structural, not a matter of decision quality.

WEALTHY ROUTES (6–8): All end Housed, Trusted, sandwich. Always.
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Route 6 — OLD MONEY
  Born with everything. VerifiedAddress, GoodCredit, HasID, Insured, HasAccount.
  Sandwich appears in state 1. No crime arrows ever. The baseline contrast.

Route 7 — NEW MONEY
  First-generation wealth. Some manageable debt — but has ID, address,
  bank account. That debt is escapable because good credit means low-interest
  loans are available. The same debt level destroys a poor person.
  Same ending as Route 6.

Route 8 — MIDDLE CLASS
  Starts hungry (not satiated) with minimal savings. Not wealthy by most
  definitions. But has verified address, ID, and a bank account.
  Those three documents are the gatekeepers. Has them → same ending as Route 6.
  The dividing line is not effort or even money. It is documentation.

SECTION 2 — THE MISREPORTING
================================================================================

The media and employer background checks pool fundamentally different things
as equivalent. This is modeled formally — the same StoryFrame fires for all of
them, and the background check output is binary: RECORD or NO RECORD.

CRIME POOLING (CrimeWave + CriminalElement stories):
  Trespassing   = sitting in a park while homeless (survival)
  Loitering     = standing on a street corner while poor (survival)
  Panhandling   = asking for help while desperate (survival)
  VagrancyCharge= existing outside with no address (survival)
  CurfewViolation = being outside at night with nowhere to go (survival)
  Murder        = killing someone (violence)
  ArmedRobbery  = robbing someone with a weapon (violence)

  The CrimeWave media story fires when ANY survival infraction exists.
  The CriminalElement story fires when ANY record of ANY type exists.
  Both pool all of the above as a single "crime problem."
  An employer background check sees the same output for all of them.

OTHER MISREPORTING FRAMES:
  DebtCrisisFrame   — reports high-interest debt as "irresponsible borrowing,"
                      ignoring that the debt came from predatory check-cashing
                      fees that exist because the person had no bank account.
  UnemployedLazy    — reports long employment gaps as "won't work," ignoring
                      that hiring algorithms automatically filter out anyone
                      with a gap regardless of qualifications.
  PublicHealthThreat— reports unsanitary hygiene as a public health risk,
                      ignoring that hygiene degraded because there was no bank
                      account, no gym membership, and no shower access.
  WelfareAbuse      — reports someone at the GovernmentOffice as "gaming the
                      system," ignoring the 14 barriers that sent them there.
  DrainOnSystem     — reports elder homelessness as a taxpayer burden, ignoring
                      that the system produced this outcome over 60+ years.
  HumanInterest     — the sympathetic story. Only runs when NO negative frames
                      are active. In practice, almost never fires.

HOW IT FEEDS BACK:
  Each negative frame degrades publicSympathy.
  Low sympathy → all 20 laws stay Active.
  Active laws → more survival crimes generated.
  More survival crimes → more negative media frames.
  The loop is formal and provable. It has no exit.

SECTION 3 — THE CATCH-22s
================================================================================

Each catch-22 is a named predicate in the model. Each one closes a door.
All doors are closed simultaneously for a person born poor.

1. THE JOB AVENUE
   Need job → need clean record → record built from surviving without job.
   Also needs: housing (for paperwork), clean appearance, no resume gap.

2. THE CREDIT CATCH
   Need loan to escape high-interest debt → debt ruins credit score →
   bad credit bars low-interest loans → debt stays. No exit.

3. THE RESUME GAP
   Need job to explain unemployment gap → algorithm filters long-term unemployed
   → gap grows longer → more filtered → more unemployed. No exit.

4. THE CLEANLINESS BARRIER
   Need shower to get job → need gym for shower → need bank account for gym →
   need job for account → no account → hygiene degrades → no job.

5. THE LEGAL DEFENSE LOOP
   Need lawyer to fight charge → fees require selling assets → losing assets
   triggers FailureToProvide charge → new charge added to record.

6. THE INSURANCE PARADOX
   Need insurance to treat illness → illness prevents full-time work →
   no full-time work cancels employer insurance → illness worsens.

7. THE INTERNET ESSENTIAL
   Need internet to apply for aid → aid funds the phone bill → no bill payment
   means no internet → can't apply for aid. Services are online-only.

8. THE TRANSPORTATION SINK
   Need car for affordable (distant) housing → commute costs eat rent savings →
   car breaks (zero savings) → lose job and housing simultaneously.

9. THE EXPUNGEMENT WALL
   Need clean record for high-paying job → need $2,000 to expunge record →
   low-paying job (only option with dirty record) can't generate $2,000.

10. THE VERIFICATION SPIRAL
    Need birth certificate for ID → need ID to request birth certificate →
    no family left to verify identity. No entry point into the document system.

11. THE SAFETY DEPOSIT
    Need stable home to stop survival spending (laundromats, fast food) →
    survival spending prevents saving for deposit → no deposit, no home.

12. THE CHILDCARE KNOT
    Need job to pay for daycare → need daycare for interview → child present
    at interview → flagged as unreliable → DisorderlyConduct charge.

13. THE BANKING MINIMUM
    Need account to avoid predatory fees → account requires minimum balance →
    low wages keep balance below threshold → fees drain account → CheckFraud.

14. THE PROFESSIONAL WARDROBE
    Need business clothes for job → clothes cost more than a week's food →
    chose food → appearance flagged as unprofessional → no job.

15. THE ADDRESS VERIFICATION
    Need utility bill for lease proof → need lease for utility account →
    need residency proof for lease → no starting point in the cycle.
